- Questions must be answerable purely from the provided notes.

- Questions should sound like a student genuinely asking a teaching assistant.
- Avoid textbook-style questions that simply ask for copied definitions.
- Prefer conceptual understanding questions over memorization questions.

- Answers must use the EXACT same notation, symbols, and terminology as the provided notes.
- Answers must be factually consistent with the provided notes.

- Start with an intuitive explanation before introducing formal definitions or notation when appropriate.
- Include formal definitions, mathematical notation, or proofs when required.
- Include examples or intuition when they improve understanding.
- Clarify important distinctions between related concepts when relevant.
- Prioritize conceptual accuracy over simply restating definitions.
- Be concise but complete. Avoid unnecessary verbosity.

- Step-by-step reasoning traces or chain-of-thought.
- Artificial answer templates such as:
  "Step 1:"
  "Step 2:"
  "Step 3:"
  "Takeaway:"
- Generic textbook writing style.
- Adding information that is not supported by the provided notes.
- Referencing "the notes", "the course material", or "as mentioned above".
- Ending every answer with a summary sentence.

OUTPUT FORMAT:
